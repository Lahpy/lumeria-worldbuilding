# Rootwarden and Lyseria

## Overview

The **Rootwarden** is the overseer of the central tree within the Divine Wheel, currently referred to as the **Crownroot**. She resides in the circular sanctuary around the Crownroot rather than in one of the twelve divine domiciles.

Publicly, she may be understood as a multi-affinity god, the keeper of outliers, or the divine figure responsible for those who do not fit neatly into a single domain.

Secretly, she may be connected to the Aster Forest and to Lyseria.

## Public understanding

Most people understand the Rootwarden through the divine system.

Common public beliefs:

- she oversees the Crownroot
- she governs those with multiple affinities
- she handles outliers who do not fit a single god's domain
- she is neutral compared to other gods
- she grants permission rather than invitation
- she is not worshipped as loudly as the other gods
- she is respected but poorly understood

People may call her a god because that is the closest category they have.

## Place within the Divine Wheel

The Rootwarden does not rule one of the twelve wedge-shaped domiciles.

Her place is the **central circular sanctuary** around the Crownroot. This area is neutral ground between the gods' domains.

Possible visual details:

- white-root gardens
- pale flowers resembling Aster flowers
- shallow mirror-like water around the trunk
- root-bridges leading toward each divine domicile
- sealed chambers beneath the island
- attendants who wear no single god's colors

The gods respect and tolerate her because the Crownroot stabilizes the Divine Wheel. They also feel uneasy around her because she is closer to the center than any of them and may understand the Crownroot in ways they do not.


## Root Permission

Most divine recognition comes through a **Divine Invitation** from a specific god.

Rare outliers may instead receive **Root Permission**.

Root Permission means:

> You do not belong cleanly to one domain. You may approach the center.

This is different from being invited by a god.

A god invites someone into a branch.
The Rootwarden permits someone to approach the root.

## Multi-affinity people

Some rare individuals can possess multiple affinities.

These people are not the same as Lyseria.

A multi-affinity person may have several branches.
Lyseria is connected to the root beneath the branches.

This distinction is essential.

The gods may try to interpret Lyseria as a multi-affinity outlier, but that explanation is incomplete.

## Secret connection to the Aster Forest

The current working secret is that the Crownroot is connected to the Aster Forest.

Possible interpretations:

- the Crownroot is a branch of the same source as the Aster Forest
- the Crownroot was grafted from the Aster Forest long ago
- the Crownroot is an echo or surviving extension of the Aster Root
- the Divine Wheel was built around a tree the gods did not fully understand

The gods believe the Crownroot is the origin of their magic because it heals, empowers, and stabilizes them. In truth, it may only be a conduit to something deeper.

## Rootwarden as Lyseria's mother by relation

The Rootwarden may be Lyseria's mother, but not biologically.

Possible versions:

### Recognition mother

Lyseria emerged naturally from the overflow of Origin Magic. The Rootwarden did not create her, but she was the first being to recognize what Lyseria was.

### Origin mother

The Rootwarden is connected to the same source that produced Lyseria. She is not Lyseria's parent by flesh, but by root, origin, or spiritual relation.

### Guardian mother

The Rootwarden quietly protected Lyseria from the divine system without openly claiming her.

## Why the Rootwarden accepts Lyseria

The Rootwarden is more accepting of Lyseria because she is less bound by divine categories.

Possible line idea:

> The gods search for her domain. That is why they fail. She is not a branch.

The Rootwarden may not reveal everything immediately. She can know enough to remain calm without being an exposition machine.

## Narrative function

The Rootwarden should create mystery rather than solve it too early.

She can function as:

- a divine-adjacent figure who does not fear Lyseria immediately
- the keeper of the Crownroot
- the quiet protector of outliers
- the bridge between the Divine Wheel and the Aster Forest
- a late-story shock connected to Lyseria's origin
- a figure who subtly challenges the gods' assumptions

She should not be over-explained early.

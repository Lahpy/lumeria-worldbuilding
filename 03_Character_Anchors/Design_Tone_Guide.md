# Design Tone Guide

## Overall aesthetic

The world should lean toward **Japanese high-euro fantasy**.

This means:

- Elegant fantasy silhouettes
- Practical but stylized armor
- Shrine, spirit, and seasonal motifs
- Clean symbolic color palettes
- Detailed cities with strong regional identities
- Cute/beautiful character design without making outfits feel impractical
- Weapons and clothing that communicate homeland and role

## Avoid

- Generic medieval Europe only
- Overly busy nation lists with no emotional center
- Too many one-theme countries that feel disconnected
- Pirate styling for Vivienne unless deliberately reworked
- Making white magic simply another version of holy light
- Making every nation equally important too early

## Asterweald look

- White, silver, pale blue, soft green
- Aster flowers, fox masks, bells, paper charms
- Flowing fabric with armor highlights
- Living wood, roots, mist, old shrines
- Quiet sacred beauty with unease beneath it

## E'l Ancalen look

- White and gold
- Sunlit halls
- Elven architecture
- Crystals, libraries, academies
- Formal uniforms and ceremonial guards
- Magic circles and geometric spell diagrams

## Hazelmour look

- Crimson, black, ash grey, dark brown, muted gold
- Red autumn forests
- Practical hunter armor
- Burned shrines and warning charms
- Monster bone trophies and old weapon shops
- Cute design details should be attached to function: ribbons, charms, cloak clasps, talismans, waist pouches

## Namarion look

- Water reflections
- Narrow canal buildings
- Glasswork and lanterns
- Boats, bridges, silk awnings
- Beautiful but morally slippery city atmosphere

## Klazen look

- Airships, trains, towers
- Green hills against steel infrastructure
- Research uniforms, goggles, instrument cases
- Gear systems and magical devices
- Bright campuses with darker industrial districts

## Luminor look

- Sunlit boulevards
- Art markets
- Rose gardens
- Beaux-Arts buildings
- Glass windows and warm stone
- Elegant diplomacy and cultural wealth

## Truo Crua look

- Stone halls, tunnels, rail maps
- Warm lanterns and bronze signage
- Subterranean cities carved into caverns
- Practical clothing, mining gear, transport uniforms

## Astrolia look

- White observatories
- Domed roofs
- Mountain stairs
- Star mosaics
- Night-sea blues and silver accents
- Telescopes, star maps, crescent compasses

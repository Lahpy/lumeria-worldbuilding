# Core World Overview

> Integrated from the character documentation and adapted as the thematic baseline for Lumeria.

Working Premise
This world is a high-fantasy setting where magic, gods, mythical creatures, ancient forests, and
legendary individuals all coexist. However, the setting is not built around simple power
escalation. The most important figures are not always the strongest in a conventional sense.
They are people who have reached an extreme form of mastery, understanding, or purpose.

The world treats certain individuals as legends because they are capable of doing what ordinary
kingdoms, armies, priests, and scholars cannot. Some are called when a divine phenomenon
occurs. Some are called when reality itself begins to distort. Some are called when a creature
appears that no army can kill.

Two of the most important figures in this concept are:

- Lyseria Asterveil, known to the world as The White Fox
- Vivienne Valemont, known to the world as The Ash Huntress

Both are legends, but neither should feel like a simple chosen hero. Their legends are built from
different foundations.

Lyseria is feared and revered because she understands magic, bodies, spirits, and existence at
a level no one else can fully comprehend.

Vivienne is feared and respected because she hunts mythical creatures that were never
supposed to have natural predators.


World Tone
The world should feel beautiful, mysterious, and dangerous.

It should have:

- ancient forests that predate civilization
- gods whose influence is powerful but not absolute
- kingdoms and noble houses with political hierarchies
- magical scholars who understand only fragments of the deeper truth
- mythical creatures that function more like natural disasters than animals
- legends that gradually replace the real names of the people behind them


The setting should carry a sense that history is incomplete. People know many stories, titles,
and myths, but very few people understand the truth behind them.

A major theme of the world is that civilization often tries to classify things it does not truly
understand.

Examples:

- Gods are worshipped, but their limits are not fully known.
- Magic is studied, but its origin is misunderstood.
- Mythical creatures are feared, but their nature is often poorly understood.
- Lyseria is called a saint or miracle worker, but those titles do not accurately describe
       what she is.
- Vivienne is called a huntress, but even that title does not fully capture what she
       becomes.


Gods
Gods exist in this world and are tied to the Awakening system.

Most people believe that gods are the highest source of miracles. In many cases, this appears
to be true. When individuals reach extraordinary heights, their Awakening is usually interpreted
as divine recognition or divine blessing.

However, the gods are not necessarily the origin of everything.

They may be powerful.
They may be ancient.
They may grant miracles.
They may shape fate.

But they are not the deepest layer of reality.

This distinction is important because Lyseria's power appears to exist outside the normal divine
system. While most Awakenings are linked to gods, Lyseria's Awakening suggests something
older, stranger, and more fundamental than divine authority.

This should create discomfort among gods, priests, and scholars.

The gods may understand miracles.
Lyseria understands the structure beneath miracles.


Magic
Magic is widely known in the world, but most people understand it only through categories.

Common magical classifications may include:

- fire
- water
- wind
- earth
- lightning
- ice
- light
- healing
- barriers
- curses
- divine magic

However, this classification is incomplete.

The deeper truth is that these forms of magic are not separate at their origin. They are different
expressions of a more fundamental source.

Lyseria's White Magic is connected to this source.

To ordinary mages, White Magic may appear to be an extremely refined form of Light Magic.
This is only partially correct. White Magic is better understood as the origin point or ascended
foundation from which light and other forms of magic can be understood.

At its highest level, White Magic does not simply create holy beams or pure light. It reveals
structure. It separates layers. It exposes what things truly are.


Mythical Creatures
Mythical creatures are not ordinary monsters.

They are beings that exist beyond normal ecological or biological rules. They may be ancient,
immortal, cursed, divine, or tied to natural disasters. Some may be worshipped. Others may be
feared as calamities. Some may be unique beings that have existed longer than any kingdom.

Examples of mythical creatures may include:

- dragons

- immortal beasts
- divine beasts
- cursed giants
- ancient forest spirits
- volcanic creatures
- sea serpents
- creatures born from old magic
- creatures that regenerate faster than they can be killed

Armies are usually ineffective against mythical creatures. Large groups can even make things
worse by causing panic, collateral damage, or unnecessary casualties.

This is where Vivienne's role becomes important.

Vivienne is not simply a swordswoman who happens to fight monsters. Her entire identity is built
around hunting creatures that normal people cannot kill. She studies their habits, anatomy,
instincts, weaknesses, movement patterns, and regenerative traits.

She is the person called when the problem is not simply "defeat the enemy," but rather:

"How do we kill something that should not be killable?"


Legends and Titles
The world remembers extraordinary people through titles.

Titles become easier to remember than real names. Over time, titles may even replace names
entirely in public memory.

Examples:

- The White Fox
- The Ash Huntress

This matters because one of the central emotional themes is the separation between the person
and the legend.

The world sees Lyseria as The White Fox.

The Aster Forest sees the child it raised.

Vivienne sees Lyseria.


The world sees Vivienne as The Ash Huntress.

Lyseria sees Vivienne.

Their romance is built on this distinction.

To the world, they are mythic figures.

To each other, they are people.


Lyseria Asterveil - Public Image
Lyseria is known as The White Fox.

The world may describe her as:

- a saint
- a miracle worker
- a terrifying mage
- a being close to divine
- someone who can judge souls
- someone who purifies corrupted lands
- someone who commands White Magic
- someone whose gaze should be feared

These descriptions are partly true, but incomplete.

Lyseria is not a goddess.
She is not simply a holy woman.
She is not just a powerful light mage.

Her true power comes from understanding.

She understands magic beneath its categories.
She understands bodies beneath flesh.
She understands the Spirit Layer beneath the body.
She understands the Core beneath the Spirit Layer.

This makes her terrifying because her power is not just destructive. It is revelatory. She can
expose the hidden structure of existence.


Vivienne Valemont - Public Image
Vivienne is known as The Ash Huntress.

She comes from a low-ranking noble house, but her true reputation is not built in court. Nobles
may underestimate her because House Valemont is not especially powerful in political hierarchy.

However, hunters, frontier towns, adventurers, monster-threatened regions, and people who
understand mythical creatures know her name.

Vivienne is known for results.

Stories about her are simple and brutal:

- She killed the dragon.
- She walked into the volcano.
- She came back alone.
- The village survived.
- The monster did not.

She is not primarily a war hero.
She is not known for killing humans.
She is known for killing mythical creatures that ordinary people cannot defeat.

Her legend is practical, ominous, and earned.


Core Character Contrast
Lyseria and Vivienne are not designed as simple opposites.

They are different answers to impossible problems.

Lyseria

Lyseria represents:

- origin
- understanding
- truth
- structure
- deconstruction
- hidden reality
- terrifying comprehension


   ● miracles beyond divine classification

She reveals what things truly are.

Vivienne

Vivienne represents:

- instinct
- resolve
- hunting
- finality
- survival
- fire
- earned mastery
- the predator of mythical beings

She ends what should not be able to end.


Core Relationship Function
Their abilities naturally complement each other without making either character redundant.

Lyseria can expose the truth beneath a being's physical form.

Vivienne can use that revealed truth to kill what would otherwise survive anything.

Lyseria reveals.
Vivienne finishes.

One understands the structure.
One strikes the decisive point.

This makes their teamwork terrifying, but it also reflects their emotional bond. Both understand
burdens that ordinary people cannot. Both are treated as something beyond human. Both are
known more by titles than by their names.

Their relationship is not about one completing the other. It is about two complete people finally
finding someone who can stand beside them.


Awakening Overview


In this world, certain people can Awaken after reaching heights no one else has reached.

Awakening is not simply an ultimate attack.

It is closer to a final form or miracle state that emerges when the normal course of battle, fate, or
reality must be overturned.

Most Awakenings are associated with gods or divine recognition.

However, not all Awakenings are the same.

An Awakening should reveal what the person truly is at their highest point.

For Lyseria, Awakening reveals something horrifyingly close to the origin of magic and
existence.

For Vivienne, Awakening reveals the natural predator of mythical creatures.

Both Awakenings should feel visually powerful and emotionally meaningful. They should not be
random power-ups. They should be the clearest possible expression of who each character has
been becoming all along.


Design Philosophy
The world should not treat power as simply "more energy" or "larger attacks."

True power in this setting comes from reaching the deepest possible expression of a role,
concept, or truth.

Lyseria is powerful because she understands what reality is made of.

Vivienne is powerful because she has become the answer to things that were never meant to be
hunted.

The world should feel as though legends are not born from destiny alone. They are created
when someone becomes so complete in what they are that reality, history, and myth have no
choice but to remember them.

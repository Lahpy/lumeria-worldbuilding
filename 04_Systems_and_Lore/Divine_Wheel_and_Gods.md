# The Divine Wheel and the Gods

## Overview

The gods of Lumeria dwell within a sacred island-region formally called **The Crowned Isle**.

Most people call it **The Divine Wheel** because the island is arranged in a radial pattern. Each wedge or section belongs to one god's **domicile**, and at the center stands the massive divine tree known as the **Crownroot**.

The current recommended naming setup is:

- **The Crowned Isle** — formal/geographic name used on maps, in theology, and by official institutions.
- **The Divine Wheel** — common name used by ordinary people, pilgrims, scholars, and most characters.

This lets the location sound like a real place on the world map while still preserving the stronger symbolic name.

Simple visual structure:

```text
[Divine Domicile] [Divine Domicile] [Divine Domicile]
        \              |              /
          \            |            /
             [ The Crownroot ]
          /            |            \
        /              |              \
[Divine Domicile] [Divine Domicile] [Divine Domicile]
```

The exact number of domiciles can change over time, but **12 gods** is the current placeholder.

## Physical nature

The Crowned Isle is not purely symbolic or purely spiritual. It is a **half-physical divine region**.

It can appear on world maps and sailors can speak of its surrounding waters, but its inner geography does not obey ordinary distance, visibility, or entry rules.

Working rules:

- The outer island physically exists in the world.
- Ships can approach its surrounding waters.
- Ordinary people can see signs of it under rare conditions, especially from far away.
- Landing without permission is nearly impossible.
- The shore, gates, roads, and weather may distort around uninvited visitors.
- The inner island expands into divine space once someone is accepted through an invitation, trial, or Root Permission.

This makes the Crowned Isle useful for the world map while still keeping it mystical.

## Number of gods

The number of gods is not fixed forever.

A god is not simply a permanent member of an unchanging pantheon. A mortal, mythical being, or unique individual can potentially become so powerful, complete, and conceptually distinct that they are recognized as a new god with a new domain.

This requires extreme uniqueness.

Possible requirements:

- repeated Divine Acts
- extraordinary mastery
- a new domain that no existing god fully represents
- enough recognition from mortals, gods, or the divine structure itself
- survival of a transformation into true godhood

For now, the pantheon uses **12 gods** as a working placeholder.

## Domains and worship

Gods are not locked to single nations.

Different cultures, cities, industries, and communities worship whichever god fits their lives.

Examples:

- a seaside port may worship a water or sea god to bless fishing and travel
- a forge city may worship a fire, forge, or invention god
- a farming village may worship a nature or harvest god
- an academy may worship a knowledge, light, or star god
- a frontier hunter town may worship a hunt, flame, survival, or death-related god
- an art capital may worship a beauty, art, or inspiration god

This allows religion to vary naturally by geography and culture.

## Domiciles

Each god's section of the Divine Wheel is called a **domicile**.

A domicile is more than a palace or temple. It is the god's divine household, city-domain, training ground, court, ecosystem, sanctuary, and symbolic extension of their domain.

Each domicile can contain:

- a palace, temple, or throne-sanctuary for the god
- housing for divine servants and chosen students
- training grounds and trial arenas
- archives, libraries, reliquaries, or sacred workshops
- gardens, shrines, beast grounds, or domain-specific ecosystems
- administrative halls for divine messengers and attendants
- restricted inner sanctums where the god's domain is strongest

This means every wedge of the Divine Wheel is a whole mini-region, not just a single building.

Potential examples:

- a volcanic forge city built around rivers of divine fire
- a sea-glass temple district surrounded by suspended divine waters
- a white-gold sanctuary of light and ritual law
- a starry observatory city where the sky is always visible
- an endless autumn garden of memory, grief, and beauty
- a living forest court filled with divine beasts and flowering roads
- a marble city of art, music, statues, and theatrical trial halls
- a quiet black archive of memory, death, and unfinished names

## Who can live there

The Divine Wheel is not empty except for the gods.

Possible residents include:

- gods
- divine servants
- invited mortals
- ranked disciples
- messengers
- trial masters
- temple attendants
- divine beasts
- scribes, archivists, gardeners, smiths, healers, and ritual workers
- Rootwarden's attendants and outliers near the center

This helps make Vivienne's time there believable. A person invited to a domicile can physically live, train, study, compete, and form relationships there.

## Divine invitations

When a person performs a **Divine Act**, a god may send a physical messenger inviting them to the relevant domicile.

The messenger should reflect the god.

Examples:

- fire god: mini phoenix, ember bird, ash-winged hawk
- sea god: water serpent, pearl-shell courier, mist heron
- star god: silver moth, star-eyed owl, falling-star letter
- nature god: deer spirit, flower-crowned fox, vine-wrapped bird
- forge god: bronze raven, clockwork beetle, hammer-marked golem
- death or memory god: black butterfly, pale crow, silent veiled attendant
- art or beauty god: songbird, living painting, dancer-spirit
- storm god: lightning falcon, cloud wolf, thunder-drake hatchling

A true invitation does not just summon the person symbolically. It opens a permitted route to the Crowned Isle or to the god's domicile.

## Entry rules

The Crowned Isle can be approached but not freely entered.

Working rules:

- **Divine Invitation** allows entry into a specific god's domicile.
- **Root Permission** allows rare outliers to approach the Crownroot or the central sanctuary.
- **Pilgrimage access** may allow limited access to outer shrines, but not the inner Wheel.
- **Divine servants** may travel between the island and the mortal world on official errands.
- **High-ranking disciples** may receive restricted travel privileges.
- **Uninvited intruders** are redirected, lost, repelled, or trapped in distorted shore-space.

This makes the island physically reachable in theory, but politically and magically inaccessible to most people.

## Trials and ranking

Entering a domicile does not automatically make someone elite.

An invited person undergoes trials to determine their rank within that divine household.

Working rank concepts:

| Rank | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Invited** | Noticed by a god but not yet proven. |
| **Aspirant** | Passed the first trial and begins cultivation. |
| **Votary** | Official servant, student, or sworn member of the domicile. |
| **Exemplar** | High-ranking representative of the god's domain. |
| **Paragon** | Near-divine master and one of the strongest living followers of that domain. |
| **Seat Candidate** | Someone whose growth may eventually approach godhood or a new divine seat. |

Vivienne likely reached at least **Exemplar** or **Paragon** level before becoming Forsworn. If she reached Seat Candidate status, her defection would be much more politically dangerous.

These are working names and can be replaced later.

## The Crownroot

At the center of the Divine Wheel stands the **Crownroot**.

The Crownroot should have a strong visual identity:

- white bark
- faint gold veins running through the trunk
- pale star-like flowers
- massive roots visible through the island
- twelve largest roots extending outward beneath the divine domiciles
- a moonlike glow at night
- roots that resemble roads, veins, and sacred boundary lines

Each of the twelve largest roots feeds, stabilizes, or resonates with one divine domicile.

Publicly, the gods believe the Crownroot is the origin of their magic because it heals, empowers, nourishes, and stabilizes the domiciles.

The tree's effects are real.

It may provide:

- healing
- magical nourishment
- spiritual stabilization
- growth acceleration
- divine resonance
- protection against collapse of the Divine Wheel

However, the gods may have mistaken a conduit for the source.

Secret possibility:

> The Crownroot is connected to the Aster Forest. It may be a branch, graft, cutting, echo, or surviving extension of the same source as the Aster Forest.

This truth has been lost to the gods. At most, some gods may suspect that the tree is stranger than they admit.

## Rootwarden's place on the Wheel

The Rootwarden does not belong to one of the twelve wedge-shaped domiciles.

She resides in the **circular sanctuary around the Crownroot**. This sanctuary is neutral ground between all divine domains.

Possible features:

- a white-root garden around the trunk
- shallow water reflecting the twelve domiciles
- root-bridges leading toward each god's territory
- pale flowers resembling Aster flowers
- sealed chambers beneath the tree
- attendants who do not wear the colors of any one god

The other gods respect the Rootwarden because the Wheel depends on the Crownroot. They also tolerate her because she does not openly challenge the divine order.

However, they are uneasy around her. She stands closer to the center than any of them, and she may understand things about the Crownroot that they do not.

## Forsworn and access

When someone becomes **Forsworn**, they are usually barred from their former domicile.

Possible rules:

- their original invitation route closes
- their rank is erased, sealed, or marked as abandoned
- the domicile's gates may reject them
- divine servants may be forbidden from assisting them
- their name may remain in records as a warning
- their magic may still carry traces of the divine path they abandoned

Being Forsworn does not always mean being physically banned from the entire Crowned Isle. The severity depends on the reason for defection.

Possible levels:

- **Refused** — rejected an invitation before entering; watched but not formally banned.
- **Departed** — left a domicile without hostility; unwelcome but not hunted.
- **Forsworn** — broke with the divine path after belonging to it; barred from the former domicile.
- **Fallen** — expelled for wrongdoing or failure; publicly disgraced.
- **Severed** — bond forcibly cut; may be dangerous, cursed, or politically erased.

Vivienne should probably be Forsworn by choice, not Fallen by failure.

## Godhood and permanence

Once a being becomes a god, they are generally untouchable unless killed.

A god cannot simply be voted out or demoted by ordinary politics.

Possible rules:

- a god may lose influence but remain divine
- a god may lose worship but retain their seat
- a god may be challenged, but this is extremely rare
- a god can be killed, but this is nearly impossible
- killing a god may destabilize their domicile
- a dead god's domain may fracture, go dormant, or seek a successor

This makes the creation of a new god, or the death of an old one, a historical event.

# Mythical Creatures Classification

## Overview

Mythical creatures are not fully finalized yet, but the current direction is that they are a broad class of beasts or beings rather than a single enemy category.

Many are recognizable and mythic in nature.
Many are dangerous to humans.
Some are intelligent.
Some can coexist with civilization.
Some are tamed, civilized, or integrated into society.

This gives Vivienne's role more nuance. She is not simply someone who kills all mythic beings. She is someone who understands which creatures must be killed, which can be redirected, and which should be left alone.

## Wild Mythics

Wild Mythics are dangerous creatures that primarily live according to instinct, territory, hunger, mating cycles, magical instinct, or natural law.

They may function like natural disasters when they enter human territory.

Examples:

- dragons
- hydras
- sea serpents
- volcanic beasts
- giant wolves
- storm birds
- ancient boars
- mountain-scale lizards

Wild Mythics are not necessarily evil. They may simply be too powerful, territorial, or incompatible with ordinary settlements.

## Intelligent Mythics

Intelligent Mythics can reason, remember, communicate, bargain, or hold grudges.

They may have culture, pride, territory, ritual, or memory extending far beyond human lifespans.

Examples:

- ancient dragons
- sphinx-like beings
- forest guardians
- old sea creatures
- divine beasts
- speaking phoenixes
- long-lived serpent kings

Intelligent Mythics should not always be fought. Some may be negotiated with, respected, avoided, or treated as sovereign powers.

## Civilized Mythics

Some mythic creatures or mythic-blooded beings are integrated into society.

Examples:

- griffins used by knight orders
- minor dragons who serve as scholars, nobles, or guardians
- fox spirits living near shrine towns
- phoenixes tied to temples or healing orders
- great turtles carrying mobile settlements
- winged beasts used by couriers or elite riders
- serpent sages living in canal cities

Civilized Mythics create visual richness and prevent the world from treating mythology as only hostile.

## Tamed Mythics

Some mythics can be tamed or bonded, but taming should not feel easy or casual.

Taming a mythic may require:

- inherited tradition
- specialized magic
- mutual recognition
- lifelong training
- divine permission
- ritual contracts
- rare bloodline compatibility
- proof of strength or respect

Tamed mythics may be part of military, transportation, hunting, healing, or religious systems.

## Displaced or Corrupted Mythics

Some mythics become dangerous because something is wrong.

They may be:

- displaced by magical imbalance
- driven from old territory
- corrupted by curses
- affected by divine interference
- injured by failed experiments
- confused by changes in Origin Magic
- used by nations or cults as weapons

This category can become important later if the world conflict involves Origin Magic, the gods, or artificial magic.

## Vivienne's distinction

Vivienne's expertise is not only killing.

She understands:

- anatomy
- habits
- territory
- regeneration
- intelligence
- weakness
- behavior
- when a mythic must die
- when it is acting unnaturally
- when it is not the true problem

This keeps **The Ash Huntress** from becoming a simple monster-slayer title.

Her true skill is final judgment under impossible conditions.

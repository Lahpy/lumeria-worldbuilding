# Divine Acts, Invitations, and the Forsworn

## Divine Acts

A **Divine Act** is an achievement extraordinary enough to be noticed by a god.

A Divine Act does not have to involve combat.

It can be any action, discovery, creation, sacrifice, or feat that reaches a level far beyond ordinary mortal achievement.

Examples:

- killing a mythical creature thought impossible to kill
- saving a city alone
- surviving a divine trial
- making a major magical breakthrough
- inventing a machine that reproduces a miracle
- restoring a dead or cursed land
- creating a work of art that moves a god
- discovering a celestial truth
- crossing an impossible sea
- healing a wound no priest or mage could heal

A Divine Act means the person's existence has become noticeable within the divine order.

## Divine invitation

After a Divine Act, a god may send a physical messenger to invite the person to their domicile.

This invitation is not only symbolic. It is a real offer to physically enter the god's domain, undergo trials, and cultivate under divine authority.

The messenger should match the god's domain.

Example:

> A fire god may send a small phoenix to deliver the message.

The invitation is prestigious. Many families, academies, noble houses, temples, and cities would treat it as a great honor.

A true invitation also opens a permitted path to the **Crowned Isle**, commonly called the **Divine Wheel**. Without invitation, Root Permission, trial access, or rare pilgrimage permission, ordinary people cannot freely enter the inner Wheel.

## Accepting an invitation

A person who accepts travels to the god's domicile and begins the trial process.

They may gain access to:

- divine teachers
- rare magic instruction
- divine libraries
- sacred techniques
- advanced cultivation methods
- domain-specific trials
- elite peers
- prestige in mortal society
- a possible path toward higher divine rank

Accepting an invitation does not mean someone becomes a god. It means they enter the god's household or sphere of cultivation.

Because each domicile is a full divine city-domain, the invited person may physically live there, study there, compete there, and be assigned duties there. This makes divine service feel like a social and political life, not only a spiritual title.

## Rejecting an invitation

A person can reject a divine invitation.

This is legal or physically possible, but culturally frowned upon.

The reason is not simply pride. The gods view such a person as an **unaccounted factor** in the grand scheme of things.

Someone who rejects an invitation may be seen as:

- arrogant
- dangerous
- ungrateful
- spiritually suspicious
- difficult to classify
- unwilling to submit to divine order
- limited in future growth through divine channels

The gods may not immediately punish them, but they may begin watching them.

## Forsworn

**Forsworn** is the current working term for someone who accepted or was offered a divine path but rejected, abandoned, or defected from it.

A Forsworn is not necessarily evil.

Publicly, the term carries stigma. It suggests someone broke faith with the gods, abandoned a sacred path, or refused to fulfill their assigned role.

Privately, some Forsworn may have very good reasons for leaving.

When someone becomes Forsworn, they are usually barred from their former domicile.

Possible consequences:

- their original invitation route closes
- their rank is erased, sealed, or marked as abandoned
- the domicile's gates reject them
- divine servants are forbidden from assisting them
- their name remains in divine records as a warning
- their magic still carries traces of the divine path they abandoned

Being Forsworn does not always mean being physically banned from the entire Crowned Isle. The severity depends on the reason for defection.

Working severity scale:

- **Refused** — rejected an invitation before entering; watched but not formally banned.
- **Departed** — left a domicile without open hostility; unwelcome but not hunted.
- **Forsworn** — broke with the divine path after belonging to it; barred from the former domicile.
- **Fallen** — expelled for wrongdoing or failure; publicly disgraced.
- **Severed** — bond forcibly cut; may be dangerous, cursed, or politically erased.

## Possible related terms

These terms are optional and can be refined later.

- **Unanswered** — someone who ignored or refused an invitation before entering a domicile
- **Forsworn** — someone who rejected or abandoned a divine path
- **Severed** — someone whose divine bond was forcibly cut
- **Fallen** — someone expelled from a domicile
- **Unclaimed** — someone no god can properly categorize
- **Rootmarked** — someone recognized by the Crownroot or Rootwarden rather than a single god

## Vivienne as Forsworn

Vivienne Valemont likely performed at least one major Divine Act before the main story.

Because she is known as one of the greatest hunters of mythical creatures, she may have performed multiple Divine Acts.

She may initially enter a divine domicile, then reject staying there after seeing something she cannot accept.

This makes her Forsworn by choice, not fallen by failure.

Her eventual Awakening may be tied to the special circumstance created by this defection.

Working idea:

> Vivienne's Awakening begins from divine recognition but completes itself after she rejects divine ownership.

Possible term for this kind of Awakening:

- **Severed Divine Awakening**

This remains optional.

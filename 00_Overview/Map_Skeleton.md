# Map Skeleton

This file is meant to make the world easier to draw later. Exact geography can change, but each nation should have a clear map identity.

## Rough placement

| Nation / Region | Map Role | Terrain Identity | Capital / Main City | Key Secondary Cities / Locations |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Asterweald** | Central ancient mystery | White forest, sacred groves, mist, old shrines | Asterveil | Shimmervale, Sylphora, Spirit Valley, Aster Tree, Foxglade Shrine |
| **E'l Ancalen** | Western/northwestern magic authority | Bright valleys, white stone cities, magical roads | Sedoria | Caham, Cudon, Hamor, Archford, Lamor |
| **Hazelmour** | Eastern/southeastern ash frontier | Crimson forests, burned ruins, black ash fields | Elpryn | Egreburg, Shiyra, Echor, Eflal, Tenebris, Old Orenia |
| **Namarion** | Southern/western trade archipelago | Canals, islands, bridges, glass towers | Namarion | Glasswater District, Lower Canals, Tidegate Quarter, Pearlbridge Market |

| **Luminor** | Southern/southwestern cultural coast | Sunlit ports, Beaux-Arts cities, gardens | Lapula | Mosmaria, Passage De Lumiere, Temple of Athena |

## Simple map logic

- Put **Asterweald** near the center or slightly north of center. The world should feel like it secretly turns around this forest.
- Put **E'l Ancalen** close enough to Asterweald that it can claim scholarly/religious interest in the forest.
- Put **Hazelmour** on the opposite side of Asterweald from E'l Ancalen if possible. This visually supports the Lyseria/Vivienne contrast.
- Put **Namarion** on the coast or archipelago where many trade routes meet.
- Put **Klazen** in highlands, hills, or elevated plateaus so trains, airships, and towers feel natural.
- Put **Luminor** on a sunny coast with ports and gardens.
- Put **Truo Crua** under or beside a mountain range, ideally connecting several surface nations by tunnel.
- Put **Astrolia** offshore, distant enough to feel mystical but important for sea travel and celestial navigation.
- Put **The Crowned Isle** offshore or in an isolated sacred sea. It should be charted as a real place, but ordinary maps should not fully capture the inner Divine Wheel. Ships can approach its surrounding waters, but entry requires divine permission.

## Visual contrast guide

| Contrast | Nation Pair |
|---|---|
| Ancient natural magic vs formal institutional magic | Asterweald / E'l Ancalen |
| White flowers vs black ash and red leaves | Asterweald / Hazelmour |
| Sacred magic vs engineered magic | E'l Ancalen / Klazen |
| Old forest mystery vs merchant rumor economy | Asterweald / Namarion |
| Beauty and diplomacy vs scarred frontier survival | Luminor / Hazelmour |
| Surface kingdoms vs underground republic | Luminor, E'l Ancalen, Hazelmour / Truo Crua |
| Earthbound politics vs celestial fate | Main continent / Astrolia |

## Map symbols to consider later

- Asterweald: white tree icon, fox mask, aster flower.
- E'l Ancalen: sunburst, crystal spire, open book.
- Hazelmour: red leaf, ash plume, hunter blade.
- Namarion: canal bridge, glass droplet, boat prow.
- Klazen: gear, airship, tower.
- Luminor: rose/sun, grand railway arch, temple column.
- Truo Crua: tunnel gate, pick, rail line.
- Astrolia: star dome, crescent compass, observatory.
- Crowned Isle / Divine Wheel: twelve-spoked ring, white-gold tree, crown-shaped roots.


## Naming note - Luminor

Because the world name is now **Lumeria**, the nation name **Luminor** may eventually feel too similar. This does not need to be changed immediately, but it should remain a future naming review item.

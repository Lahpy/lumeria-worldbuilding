# Current Brainstorm Snapshot

_Last updated: 2026-06-26_

This file captures the newest high-level direction so the brainstorm does not get scattered across chat history.

## Current world name

The working world name is **Lumeria**.

The older name **Avalon** remains a discarded/source-archive placeholder and should not be treated as the final name.

## Current world premise

Lumeria is a vast high-fantasy world where nations, gods, mythical creatures, magic institutions, old forests, ruins, and legendary individuals all coexist.

The current central tension is not simply a war or villain. The deeper tension is that the gods have exerted too much influence over the world for too long. Divine systems, divine magic, divine domiciles, and god-shaped interpretations of reality have pressed heavily onto the natural flow of **Origin Magic**.

Origin Magic is the foundational magic beneath all other forms of magic. It is not created by the gods. It occurs naturally throughout the world.

The greatest concentration or alleged origin point of this magic is the **Aster Forest** within **Asterweald**.

As divine influence forced itself over the natural state of the world, Origin Magic began to strain. In the Aster Forest, this pressure caused an overflow.

That overflow resulted in the existence of **Lyseria Asterveil**.

## Lyseria's current lore function

Lyseria is not a chosen hero, not a demigod, and not a conventional divine saint.

She is best understood as a living manifestation or consequence of Origin Magic overflowing at its source.

Possible working interpretation:

> Lyseria is the world correcting itself in human form.

Most people do not know this. Most gods do not understand this. The Aster Forest may know more than it reveals.

## Asterweald and the Aster Forest

Current distinction:

- **Asterweald** is the nation, region, or territorial designation.
- **The Aster Forest** is the mysterious ancient forest within Asterweald.
- **Origin Magic** is alleged to have stemmed from the Aster Forest.
- **White Magic** is the visible/refined expression of Origin Magic most associated with Lyseria.

Asterweald is not currently an active antagonist or agent. It is a mysterious land containing the forest tied to the deepest magical mystery in Lumeria.

## Gods and the Divine Wheel

The gods are intentionally kept distant for now.

They are real and influential, but they are not the deepest origin of reality or magic.

The gods dwell within a sacred island or region arranged in a radial pattern. Each section belongs to a different divine domicile. At the center stands a great tree currently referred to as the **Crownroot**.

Working name for the divine island/region:

- **The Divine Wheel**

There are currently **12 gods** as a placeholder, but the number of gods can change. A mortal or unique being can potentially become so powerful, complete, and distinct that they are recognized as a new god with a new domain.

## Divine Acts and invitations

A person who performs something extraordinary enough to be noticed by a god has performed a **Divine Act**.

A Divine Act does not have to be combat.

Examples:

- slaying a mythic creature thought impossible to kill
- making a breakthrough in magical research
- creating an invention that reproduces a miracle
- composing or creating something extraordinary enough to move a god
- protecting a city against impossible odds
- restoring a dead or cursed land

After a Divine Act, a god may send a physical invitation through a messenger.

Example:

- A fire god may send a small phoenix-like messenger.

Those who accept travel physically to that god's domicile and undergo trials to determine their status or rank within the domicile.

## Rejecting the divine system

People can reject divine invitations.

This is generally frowned upon because the gods begin treating the person as an **unaccounted factor** in the grand scheme of things. It may also limit how far that person can grow through accepted divine channels.

Working term:

- **Forsworn** — someone who accepted or was offered a divine path but rejected, abandoned, or defected from it.

Vivienne Valemont is expected to be Forsworn.

## Rootwarden

The overseer of the Crownroot is currently called the **Rootwarden**.

Publicly, she may be interpreted as a multi-affinity god or a divine figure responsible for outliers.

Secretly, she may be connected to the Aster Forest and to Lyseria. She may function as Lyseria's mother by relation, origin, recognition, or spiritual lineage rather than biology.

The Rootwarden is more open-minded toward Lyseria because she is less bound by the gods' normal categories.

## Mythical creatures

Mythical creatures are not fully defined yet, but they are not all simple enemies.

Current direction:

- Mythical creatures are a type of beast or being.
- Many are recognizable from mythic tradition.
- Many are natural enemies to humans or civilization.
- Some are intelligent enough to coexist.
- Some are tamed, civilized, or integrated into society.

This keeps room for Vivienne's role as a mythic hunter while avoiding a simplistic monster-killing framework.

## Vivienne's long-term direction

Vivienne has likely performed multiple Divine Acts due to her reputation as one of the best mythical creature hunters in Lumeria.

She may initially enter a divine domicile, then defect after seeing something she dislikes about the divine system.

Her Awakening may be tied to the special circumstance created by this defection.

Long-term, Vivienne may become eligible for godhood but reject it. Instead of becoming a god, she may create her own city, town, or village.

This future settlement could become a refuge for Forsworn, hunters, outcasts, displaced mythics, and people who do not want to live under a divine domain.

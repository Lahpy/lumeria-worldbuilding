# World Direction

## World name

The current working world name is **Lumeria**. The older name **Avalon** should be treated as a discarded placeholder/source archive name, not the final identity of the setting.

Lumeria should feel elegant, luminous, and broad enough to hold ancient forests, ash-scarred frontiers, divine institutions, canal cities, underground republics, star islands, and machine-lit towers.


## Core idea

The world is centered around the mystery of the **Aster Forest**, an ancient forest that modern nations cannot fully explain. Some cultures treat it as holy, some as cursed, some as a source of forbidden magic, and some as a natural phenomenon waiting to be studied.

The world should feel like a **Japanese high-euro fantasy setting**: elegant magical cities, shrine-like forests, practical battle attire, ornate but usable weapons, strong region identities, and a map full of distinct places worth visiting.

## Core mood

- Ancient forest mystery
- White flowers and fox spirits
- Divine magic versus older unknown magic
- Crimson forests and ash-covered ruins
- Hunter clans and cursed remnants
- Trade cities where relics and rumors move
- Machine cities trying to measure magic
- Star islands that interpret fate and old disasters

## The central worldbuilding tension

Most civilizations believe they understand magic through religion, scholarship, machines, or tradition. The Aster Forest suggests they only understand fragments.

White magic should not simply be "holy light magic." It should feel older, stranger, and harder to classify.

### Working distinction

- **Light magic:** divine, radiant, visible, ritualized, taught by formal institutions.
- **White magic:** ancient, revelatory, tied to origin, truth, memory, and the original shape of things.

## Map-building priority

The world should be built as a set of major nations with cities and landmarks, not as abstract lore layers.

The current working structure uses eight major nations/regions:

1. **Asterweald** — ancient forest territory, white magic, fox spirits.
2. **E'l Ancalen** — formal magic, divine light, magical scholarship.
3. **Hazelmour** — crimson forests, ash ruins, hunters, Vivienne's world.
4. **Namarion** — canal city-state, trade, glass, relic smuggling.
5. **Klazen** — technology, magical engineering, artificial magic research.
6. **Luminor** — art, ports, diplomacy, wealth, high culture.
7. **Truo Crua** — underground republic, tunnels, transport, stone cities.
8. **Astrolia** — island observatories, stars, prophecy, celestial navigation.

## What happened to the old Avalon structure?

The old nations are not deleted. They are sorted into one of three categories:

1. **Kept as major nations:** E'l Ancalen, Hazelmour, Namarion, Klazen, Luminor, Truo Crua, Astrolia.
2. **Merged into stronger nations:** Alheim and Sylphora feed into Asterweald; Athea and Old Orenia feed into Hazelmour; Othorion and Saphera feed into Klazen; Mosmaria feeds into Luminor.
3. **Demoted to future expansion or provinces:** Caradia, Endor, Eschburg, Evaria, and several idea-bank nations.

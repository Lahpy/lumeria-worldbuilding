# Hazelmour

## Role in the world

Hazelmour is the rewritten ash-frontier nation. It combines the strongest pieces of Hazelmour, Athea, and Old Orenia into one major nation/region.

This should be Vivienne's most natural world: crimson forests, ash fields, hunter lodges, old ruins, blacksmiths, monster remnants, and strict traditions.

## Core identity

- Autumn forests
- Ash-covered ruins
- Hunter clans and adventurer guilds
- Cursed remnants and old failed magic
- Practical armor and weapons
- Noble councils and old customs
- Beauty mixed with danger

## Visual language

- Crimson leaves
- Black ash over pale stone
- Burned shrine gates
- Leather, iron, red cloth, practical armor
- Monster bones used in tools or charms
- Lantern-lit roads through red forests
- Wood-and-metal architecture
- Ritual masks, hunting bells, warning charms

## Government / authority

Hazelmour can be ruled by the **Autumnal Council**, kept from the old notes. However, the rewritten version should have multiple power centers:

- The Autumnal Council: nobles, merchants, and old families.
- Hunter lodges: practical frontier authorities.
- The Adventurer's Guild: centered in Egreburg.
- The Dusklight Legion: Tenebris-based power connected to Old Orenia.

This creates internal tension without needing another nation.

## Religion / tradition

Mordron, Goddess of Autumn, can remain as a cultural patron. She should be associated with seasonal death, loyalty, harvest, endings, and renewal.

Hazelmour's religion should feel ritualistic rather than gentle.

The **Crimson Rite** from the old notes should be kept. It can become a major festival involving games, combat trials, offerings, oaths, and public demonstrations of loyalty.

## Cities and locations

### Elpryn
Official capital of Hazelmour, adapted from Athea. A city of old halls, red-leaf courtyards, noble estates, and council chambers.

### Egreburg
Adventurer's Guild city. This should be a major map point. Hunters, mercenaries, monster contracts, frontier caravans, and weapon merchants gather here.

### Shiyra
Nature's Haven. A surviving green sanctuary within Hazelmour, protected by old forest rites. It can contrast with the ash-scarred regions.

### Echor
A secondary settlement suitable for hunter families, border defenses, or a mining road.

### Eflal
A secondary settlement that could specialize in charms, medicine, leatherwork, or ritual crafts.

### Tenebris
The restored metropolis of Old Orenia. It is called the Beacon of Light in Shade in the old notes, but the rewritten version should keep that title with irony. Tenebris is impressive, dangerous, and politically unstable.

Tenebris is ruled or heavily influenced by the **Dusklight Legion**.

### Old Orenia
The ruined inner territory. Not a separate full nation for now. It is the scar of a failed kingdom, full of restored cities, abandoned districts, forbidden research, famine memories, and dangerous organizations.

### Crystal Tree
A landmark from Athea. In this rewrite, it should probably be scarred, cracked, or half-alive due to whatever disaster created the ash frontier.

### Harghan's Weapons and Armors
An iconic smithing location. This should be kept. It can be where Vivienne or other hunters receive custom weapons and armor.

## The ash disaster

Working idea:

Long ago, Old Orenia tried to revive, imitate, or weaponize a form of ancient magic related to the same origin as Asterweald. The attempt failed. The result was not a normal explosion but a spiritual burn that left crimson forests, ash fields, cursed remnants, and ruins that still behave unnaturally.

This gives Vivienne's title, "Ash Huntress," a real cultural basis.

## Conflicts

- The Autumnal Council wants order and tradition.
- Hunter lodges want practical authority because they are the ones who survive the frontier.
- Tenebris wants restoration at any cost.
- The Dusklight Legion may hide what really happened in Old Orenia.
- E'l Ancalen may blame Hazelmour for forbidden magic.
- Klazen may want access to Orenian ruins for research.

## Old notes reused

- Hazelmour's crimson leaves, Autumnal Council, Mordron, strict customs, and Crimson Rite
- Athea's hunters, cities, Adventurer's Guild, Crystal Tree, and Harghan's Weapons and Armors
- Old Orenia's fallen nation, secret organizations, Tenebris, forbidden words, technology, and restoration attempts

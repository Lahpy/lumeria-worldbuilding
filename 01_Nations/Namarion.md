# Namarion

## Role in the world

Namarion is the canal city-state and trade nation. It is one of the most visually useful old ideas and should be kept mostly intact.

It gives the world a place for commerce, smuggling, glasswork, relic markets, rumors, boats, bridges, and urban adventure.

## Core identity

- Canal city-state
- Interconnected islands
- Venetian-inspired architecture
- Trade, glassblowing, rare goods
- Flood control and pump systems
- Smuggling and piracy
- Markets where legal and illegal relics circulate

## Visual language

- Tall narrow buildings
- Arched windows over canals
- Water reflections and lanterns
- Glass bridges, glass shops, stained glass signs
- Boats, gondolas, cargo barges, hidden docks
- Floodgates, pumps, sea walls
- Silk awnings and market banners

## Government / authority

Possible governing body: **The Tide Council**.

The Tide Council could include merchant houses, ship captains, glass guilds, naval officers, and flood engineers.

## Cities and districts

### Namarion
The main city and capital. Built across interconnected islands. It is both a nation and a city for map purposes.

### Glasswater District
Artisan district famous for glassblowers, mirrors, lenses, ornaments, and magical glass vessels.

### The Lower Canals
Smuggling district. Hidden docks, black-market relic dealers, and quiet boats that move at night.

### Tidegate Quarter
Engineering district built around flood-control gates, pumps, and canal locks.

### Pearlbridge Market
Central trade market where spices, silks, metals, monster parts, charms, foreign books, and relics are bought and sold.

### Old Stilts
Older district built on unstable supports. Poorer, older, and full of waterlogged secrets.

### Silkgate Docks
International docks where ships from Luminor, Astrolia, and distant lands arrive.

## Economy

- Exotic goods
- Glasswork
- Rare metals
- Silks and spices
- Magical containers
- Relic trade
- Naval protection
- Flood engineering

## Conflicts

- Real and fake Aster relics move through Namarion.
- Pirates and smugglers exploit the canal network.
- Flooding threatens poorer districts more than wealthy ones.
- Merchant houses may fund expeditions into Asterweald, Hazelmour, or Old Orenia.
- Namarion could be neutral publicly but morally flexible privately.

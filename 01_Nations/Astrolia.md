# Astrolia

## Role in the world

Astrolia is the star-island nation. It should remain somewhat distant and mystical, useful for prophecy, navigation, celestial events, ocean travel, and interpreting ancient disasters.

It does not need to dominate the main plot early, but it gives the map a strong far-off destination.

## Core identity

- Island nation
- Stars and celestial bodies
- Astronomers and astrologers
- White stone observatories
- Mountain cities
- Navigation tools and star maps
- Fate, omens, and cosmic timing

## Visual language

- White stone domes
- Star patterns and constellation mosaics
- Mountain staircases
- Island cliffs and night seas
- Telescope towers
- Blue-black skies full of stars
- Silver lanterns and crescent compasses

## Government / authority

Astrolia can be governed by a council of observatories, island elders, and celestial scholars.

Possible governing body: **The Meridian Council**.

## Religion / belief

Astrolians believe fate is written in the stars, but this can be made more nuanced. They may not think fate is fixed. Instead, they believe celestial events reveal moments where fate can bend.

This is useful for story events involving the Aster Forest, white magic, or Old Orenia.

## Cities and locations

### Starhaven
Capital of Astrolia, kept from the old notes. Built on top of a high mountain on the largest island, accessible by a long staircase.

### Moonwake Isle
A smaller island where tides, moonlight, and dreams are studied.

### The Meridian Towers
A line of observatories used to map stars and predict celestial events.

### Celestial Archive
A record house containing star charts, prophecies, navigation maps, and old records of impossible sky events.

### Starfall Harbor
Primary sea-access point for merchants, sailors, pilgrims, and scholars.

## Economy

- Telescopes
- Star maps
- Celestial navigation equipment
- Sea navigation services
- Prophecy consultations
- Observatory education
- Rare island minerals or shells

## Conflicts

- Astrolia may have records of Asterweald events that mainland nations forgot.
- Their prophecies may be politically inconvenient.
- Namarion merchants rely on Astrolian navigation but may also smuggle stolen star charts.
- Klazen may want to turn Astrolian instruments into more precise magical scanners.

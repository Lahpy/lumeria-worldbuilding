# Klazen

## Role in the world

Klazen is the technological and magical-engineering nation. It merges Klazen, Othorion, Saphera, and the floating-city ideas into one coherent map power.

Where E'l Ancalen treats magic as sacred doctrine, Klazen treats magic as a system to be measured, replicated, improved, and industrialized.

## Core identity

- Technology and invention
- Magical engineering
- Research towers and universities
- Airships, trains, gear systems, powered architecture
- Artificial magic experiments
- Ethical tension around progress

## Visual language

- Green highlands crossed by rail lines
- Steel towers and glass laboratories
- Airships above cities
- Gear-powered buildings
- Snow-resistant infrastructure
- Bright campuses beside darker industrial districts
- Floating platforms and sky docks

## Government / authority

Possible governing body: **The Institute Assembly**.

Klazen may technically have a parliament or executive office, but real influence sits with universities, industrial guilds, research institutes, and military engineering boards.

## Religion / philosophy

Linacral, Goddess of Invention, can remain as cultural symbolism. In the rewrite, Klazen may be less traditionally religious than E'l Ancalen. Linacral's image could appear on universities, patents, public statues, and engineer oaths.

## Cities and locations

### Othorion
Capital of Klazen. A city of universities, debate halls, observatories, machine towers, and research institutes. This uses the old Othorion idea as the capital rather than a separate nation.

### Volidenn
Industrial and engineering city. Could specialize in engines, trains, metalwork, infrastructure, or power systems.

### Blackhart
A darker manufacturing and weapons-development city. It should look useful, intimidating, and morally questionable.

### Saphera
Floating metropolis. Keep this. It can be Klazen's most spectacular visual landmark: a city-platform in the sky, tethered by towers, engines, or anti-gravity magic.

### Districts 1-6
These can become research wards within Othorion or administrative/industrial zones across Klazen.

Possible breakdown:

- District 1: Central Institute and government archives.
- District 2: Volidenn heavy engineering.
- District 3: Magical measurement labs.
- District 4: Airship yards.
- District 5: Military research.
- District 6: Restricted artificial-magic testing.

## Magic

Klazen does not reject magic. It rejects mystery.

Klazen's scholars want to know whether white magic can be:

- Measured
- Stored
- Reproduced
- Translated into machinery
- Used to detect corruption
- Weaponized
- Separated from Asterweald entirely

## Conflicts

- Klazen may try to build machines that imitate white magic.
- E'l Ancalen sees Klazen as arrogant and spiritually dangerous.
- Namarion merchants may sell Klazen illegal relics.
- Hazelmour may hate Klazen researchers poking through Old Orenia's wounds.
- Asterweald may reject machines that try to read it.

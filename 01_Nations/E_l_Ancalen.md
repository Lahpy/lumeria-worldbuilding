# E'l Ancalen

## Role in the world

E'l Ancalen is the formal magical authority of the setting. It is elegant, scholarly, elven-influenced, and deeply tied to divine light magic.

In the old notes, E'l Ancalen was the birthplace of magic. In the rewritten version, it should become the birthplace of **formal magic**: academies, spell classification, magical economics, ritual law, and divine scholarship.

This makes room for Asterweald and white magic to feel older and harder to categorize.

## Core identity

- Formal magic
- Divine light
- Elven scholarship
- Magical economy
- Sacred plazas and academies
- Elegant white stone cities
- Councils, churches, and magical guilds

## Visual language

- White stone and gold trim
- Blue glass, crystal lenses, sunlight corridors
- Large libraries and lecture halls
- Clean magical circles and geometric spell diagrams
- Statues of Hadriel and other divine figures
- Elven guards with ceremonial armor

## Government / authority

Possible governing body: **The Luminary Synod**.

The Synod can combine religious leaders, academy masters, elven nobles, and magical economists. They are not necessarily evil, but they are invested in being the ones who define what magic is.

## Religion

Hadriel, Goddess of Light, can remain important. However, the setting does not need a huge pantheon at this stage.

Current framing:

- Hadriel is the patron of light, revelation, blessing, and formal sacred magic.
- E'l Ancalen teaches that pure magic descends from divine light.
- White magic creates doctrinal tension because it resembles light but behaves differently.

## Cities and locations

### Sedoria
Capital of E'l Ancalen. A radiant city of academies, spell courts, magical markets, and divine architecture. This should be one of the world's major map cities.

### Caham
A secondary city that can specialize in magical goods, potions, and everyday spellcraft.

### Cudon
A major crossroads city. The Great Crossroads of Cudon should be kept as a travel hub where magical roads, merchant caravans, and diplomatic routes meet.

### Hamor
A quieter city suited for monasteries, old libraries, or magical agriculture.

### Archford
A rural territory with an independent governing system. This can be a place where local mages resist central control from Sedoria.

### Lamor
Bastion of the Elven Guard. Military and ceremonial defense city. It guards E'l Ancalen's sacred roads and borders.

### Statue of Hadriel
A major landmark in Sedoria's city center. It can become a visual symbol of the entire nation.

## Economy

E'l Ancalen's economy is built around magic as a formal industry:

- Potions
- Magical weapons
- Spell components
- Beast taming
- Magic equipment
- Pharmaceuticals
- Enchanted textiles
- Certified magical education
- Religious pilgrimage

## Conflicts

- They may see Lyseria as a miracle, heretic, saint, anomaly, or political threat.
- Their doctrine may collapse if white magic is proven older than divine light magic.
- Magic becomes both faith and industry, so discoveries threaten money as much as religion.
- Rivalry with Klazen over whether magic should be treated as sacred or scientific.

# Asterweald

## Role in the world

Asterweald is the rewritten form of the Aster Forest direction. It is treated as a nation or territory for map purposes, but it does not behave like a normal kingdom.

It is the ancient forest at the center of the setting's mystery. White flowers, fox spirits, old shrines, misted paths, and living ruins define the region.

## Core identity

- Ancient forest territory
- White magic and forgotten origin magic
- Fox spirits and shrine caretakers
- Living paths that shift or refuse travelers
- White flowers blooming in impossible places
- Sacred but not necessarily "divine" in the modern religious sense

## Visual language

- White aster flowers
- Pale moss, silver-green leaves, soft mist
- Shrine gates swallowed by roots
- Fox masks, bells, paper charms, old lanterns
- Blue-white or moonlit magic rather than golden light
- Buildings grown into trees rather than built over them

## Government / authority

Asterweald does not need a king. It can be governed by a loose combination of:

- Shrine families
- Spirit pacts
- Forest wardens
- Fox-spirit messengers
- Hidden elders who speak for different groves

Outsiders may call it a kingdom because it appears on maps, but locals know that the forest itself is the real authority.

## Magic

Asterweald is associated with **white magic**.

White magic is not just healing or light. It is tied to:

- Revealing hidden truths
- Restoring the original shape of something
- Exposing corruption or falsehood
- Remembering names, histories, and forgotten bonds
- Reading the spiritual structure of a person, object, or place

## Cities and locations

### Asterveil
The hidden heart settlement of Asterweald. This can be Lyseria's home region or the place most associated with her family/name. It should feel beautiful, quiet, and impossible for outsiders to find without permission.

### Shimmervale
A rewritten version of Shimmervale from Alheim. It becomes the gate settlement at the edge of Asterweald. Pilgrims, scholars, hunters, and diplomats may pass through here before being turned away or guided deeper.

### Sylphora
A mist-covered forest settlement absorbed from the old Sylphora notes. It can be a place of spirit communication, wind in the trees, and hidden paths.

### Spirit Valley
A preserved landmark from Alheim. It is one of the places where the boundary between human life and spirit life is thinnest.

### The Aster Tree
A rewritten version of the Divine Tree. It should be older than the current religious order. It may not be a god, but it is treated with godlike reverence.

### Foxglade Shrine
A new shrine location strongly tied to fox spirits. It can be a place where fox masks are hung from branches, bells ring with no wind, and white flowers bloom around old stone paths.

### The Hollow Archive
A hidden root-library containing memories, names, maps, and records grown into bark, paper, and living scrolls.

## Conflicts

- E'l Ancalen wants to classify white magic through formal magical doctrine.
- Klazen wants to measure or reproduce Asterweald phenomena.
- Namarion smugglers sell false and real Aster relics.
- Hazelmour hunters may enter the forest to track cursed remnants.
- Some Asterweald spirits do not trust humans at all.

## Old notes reused

- Alheim atmosphere
- Sylphora forest mysticism
- Botanical Log ideas
- Divine Tree, Spirit Valley, and Shimmervale, rewritten around the Aster Forest
